This distribution reflects the clinician's assessment of likely outcomes based on the available clinical picture. It is a communication aid, not a validated prognostic calculator. Probability estimates carry inherent uncertainty — particularly in the better-outcome tail.

Outcome scale: Left to right represents increasing functional recovery. The DOC states (Coma, UWS, MCS, MCS+, eMCS) are all functionally equivalent to mRS 5 (bedridden, full nursing care) but are distinguished here by level of awareness, which carries prognostic and ethical significance. The rightmost bin ("Baseline") is anchored to this patient's premorbid functional state — not an idealized full recovery.

Modified Rankin Scale descriptions (Rankin 1957, van Swieten et al. 1988): mRS 0 no symptoms; mRS 1 no significant disability despite symptoms; mRS 2 slight disability, independent; mRS 3 moderate disability, walks without assistance; mRS 4 moderately severe, unable to walk or attend to bodily needs without assistance; mRS 5 severe disability, bedridden, incontinent, requires nursing care.
