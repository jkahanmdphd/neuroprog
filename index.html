Disorders of consciousness — definitions

Coma Complete absence of arousal and awareness. Eyes closed, no sleep-wake cycles, no purposeful response to stimulation.
UWS / VS Unresponsive wakefulness syndrome (formerly vegetative state). Arousal without awareness — eyes may open spontaneously, sleep-wake cycles are present, but there is no reproducible evidence of conscious perception of self or environment.
MCS− Minimally conscious state without language. Minimal, reproducible but inconsistent signs of awareness — such as visual pursuit, localisation to pain, or contingent affect — without command following, intelligible verbalisation, or intentional communication.
MCS+ Minimally conscious state with language. As MCS−, but with evidence of language function: command following, intelligible verbalisation, or intentional communication. Any one of these is sufficient to indicate MCS+.
eMCS Emerged from minimally conscious state. Consistent functional communication (reliably answering yes/no questions) or functional object use (using common objects appropriately), indicating the patient has crossed the upper boundary of the disorder of consciousness spectrum.

DOC definitions adapted from Giacino et al. (2020). Disorders of consciousness after acquired brain injury: the state of the science. Nature Reviews Neurology, 18, 535–550. doi:10.1038/s41582-020-00428-x

Modified Rankin Scale — definitions

mRS 5 Severe disability. Bedridden, incontinent, and requires constant nursing care and attention. All DOC states above are functionally equivalent to mRS 5 but are distinguished by level of awareness, which carries independent prognostic and ethical significance.
mRS 4 Moderately severe disability. Unable to walk without assistance and unable to attend to own bodily needs without assistance.
mRS 3 Moderate disability. Requires some help, but able to walk without assistance.
mRS 2 Slight disability. Unable to carry out all previous activities, but able to look after own affairs without assistance.
mRS 1 No significant disability despite symptoms. Able to perform all usual duties and activities.
mRS 0 No symptoms.

Modified Rankin Scale adapted from Rankin (1957) and van Swieten et al. (1988).
